Last Saturday, on June 06, something memorable happened to me at the camping park. 

At that time, I was playing archery with my friends. Take an arrow and shoot to the target. It was simply a normal day... until the unluckiest moment happen to me when I run and... KRRK!

One of the wood planks broke out when I step on it. My left foot went in and... CLANG! My shin got hit on an old metal.

The skin got rip and blood came out, making most people around me panic. They pour water and put iodine solution for an emergency heal. Deep in my heart, I was worried because I have never experience this throughout my whole life. But I try to calm myself down and saying to myself that everything is going to be fine.

Few minutes later, one of my friends said to me that there is a chance that I got [Tetanus](https://www.cdc.gov/tetanus/about/index.html). In short, Tetanus is a rare disease that can be found in the environment. In this case, the rust of the metal could have tetanus-causing bacteria on it.

So within a minute, I decided to see the doctor in the hospital.

Together with my friends to the hospital, I went to the emergency room.
