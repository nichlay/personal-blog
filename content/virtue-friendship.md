A few months ago, I took a general education class about humanity (basically a philosophy class). One of the books I read was Nicomachean Ethics by Aristotle. Among all the ideas in that book, one concept stayed with me the most: virtue friendship.

Virtue friendship is not just about having fun together or helping each other when it is convenient. It is simply a friendship where two or more people genuinely care for each other’s growth, character, and well-being. The kind of friendship that truly values each other as a person.

When I first read about this, I started imagining what it would be like to have that kind of friendship. A few companions who stay close, grow together, and look out for one another. Isn’t that a beautiful thing?

But then I thought about real life. Look at the people around you. How many of them can you truly have deep conversations with? How many people make you feel understood, challenged, and supported at the same time?

Probably not many.

And that is what makes virtue friendship so rare and beautiful. It reminds me that it is better to have a few close friends who truly know you than many general friends who only know the surface of your life.

Most people say we need more connections, and I agree. Connections are important. But when it comes to close friendship, I believe virtue friendship is the kind that can stay until the end.

It is not easy to find, but when we do, it is something worth protecting.
